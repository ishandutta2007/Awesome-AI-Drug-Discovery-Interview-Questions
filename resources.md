# Additional Resources

A short list of resources for going deeper on AI-driven drug discovery. (Not exhaustive — PRs welcome.) This field moves quickly and spans two disciplines (ML and life sciences), so cross-checking anything specific against current literature before an interview is worthwhile.

## Foundational Reading
- *Deep Learning for the Life Sciences* — Ramsundar, Eastman, Walters, Pande. A solid, hands-on introduction bridging cheminformatics and modern deep learning.
- *An Introduction to Medicinal Chemistry* — Graham Patrick. Useful for building foundational medicinal chemistry vocabulary if coming from a pure ML background.
- Reviews on structure-based drug design and the role of deep learning-based structure prediction tools in modern discovery pipelines — search recent (last 1-2 years) review articles in journals like *Nature Reviews Drug Discovery*, since this area evolves quickly.

## Key Benchmark Datasets & Platforms (good to be conversationally familiar with)
- MoleculeNet — a widely used benchmark suite for molecular property prediction tasks.
- ChEMBL and PubChem — large public bioactivity databases commonly used for training and benchmarking.
- The Protein Data Bank (PDB) — the primary repository of experimentally solved protein structures, foundational for structure-based work.

## Cheminformatics & ML Tooling
- RDKit — the standard open-source cheminformatics toolkit (fingerprints, descriptors, canonicalization); hands-on familiarity is often assumed.
- Familiarity with at least one graph neural network framework commonly used for molecular property prediction is generally expected for ML-focused roles.

## Communities & Discussion
- r/chemistry, r/MachineLearning, and cheminformatics-focused mailing lists/forums for discussion threads.
- RDKit and MoleculeNet community forums/GitHub discussions for practical, tooling-level questions.

## Staying Current
- Follow recent publications and preprints (bioRxiv, ChemRxiv, arXiv's q-bio category) from major academic labs and industry groups actively publishing in this space — new structure prediction, generative design, and validation methodology papers appear frequently and shift best practices.
- Regulatory guidance on AI/ML in drug development is actively evolving in multiple jurisdictions — check the relevant regulatory body's current published guidance directly if a role involves work close to the regulatory-facing end of a program, rather than relying on this repo or older sources.
